The best Chicken Pot Pie

What's a pie without chicken in it...? Well it isn't a chicken pot pie that's for sure. Follow this chicken pot pie recipe to absolutely blow away your dinner guests.

This is Grandmothers finest chicken pot pie recipe, to eat this is to experience not even one tenth the power of Grandmothers. Yes, it is just that damn good. Make sure to prepare the ingredients in advance and to pay attention to the cooking times. Don't want to keep those guests waiting now do we? (right..?). So crack that bottle of wine you have been eyeing while reading this and get ready to dive into the culinary equivalent of a winning lottery ticket. As another reminder keep this recipe as a general guideline, Grandma didn't always have fresh ingredients laying around. She used what she had.

Ingredients

  • Chicken

    Boneless and skinless breast cut into small cubes

  • Vegetables

    Whatever you can get your hands on really. Some good ones to throw in include:
    • Mushrooms
    • Peas
    • Carrots
    • Onions
    • Celery
    • Broccoli
    • Onions

  • Butter and Flour

    These will be the base of the roux (sauce).

  • Seasonings

    Salt, pepper, and celery seed will form a great base to go off. Add whatever else you'd like to taste.

  • Broth and Milk

    Broth and milk will deepen the flavour of your dish. Milk is also used in the roux.

  • Pie Crusts

    Two unbaked crusts are needed. You are more than welcome to make your own however store bought is perfectly fine.

  • A Drink to Enjoy (optional but highly recommended)

Cooking Steps

  1. Make the Filling

    Boil the cubed chicken with your Vegetables (Not the onions!) for about 15 minutes. Drain and set aside. Cook the onions in butter till they are translucent, then stir in the flour and seasonings. Add chicken broth and milk and let it simmer until it is at your desired thickness. Keep the cooked veggies and the sauce separate for now.

  2. Fill and Bake the Pie

    Place the chicken veggie mix in an unbaked pie crust. Pour the chicken broth mixture overtop. Cover top with the top crust, seal the edges (pinching works good), and cut slits in the top (to let steam escape). Bake until the pastry is a golden brown and filling is bubbly.

  3. Drink Wine and Bask

    You're basically done already! Sit back, wait for the pie to crisp up, and enjoy!
